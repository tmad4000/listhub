## Long version (~240 words — blog-style forward)

> Want to get **Jacob Cole** in front of a few of you. He's building
> **IdeaFlow Memory** — an automatic company memory layer for fast-growing
> startups.
>
> The premise: in 2026, every company's real source of truth is Slack. That's
> where decisions get made, customer insights land, and processes emerge. But
> nothing reliably captures that memory in a form the team (or an AI agent)
> can actually use. Manual wikis go stale in weeks. Notion is where intentions
> go to die. AI enterprise search gives bad answers because the knowledge was
> never safely written down in the first place.
>
> The problem isn't retrieval. It's **safe capture**. Jacob's built a small but
> unusual primitive that unlocks this: the agent classifies visibility per item
> **at write time**, as a first-class API field. Leadership decisions stay
> scoped to leadership. Company-wide SOPs are queryable by everyone. Sensitive
> customer context stays with the people who should see it. No manual tagging,
> no poisoned vault.
>
> IdeaFlow Memory ingests Slack first (optionally email, Drive, local files),
> extracts structured knowledge continuously, and lets people ask questions from
> surfaces they already use: a Slack bot, iMessage as a remote control, or
> their own Claude.
>
> He's running a handful of design partner pilots with 30–100 person companies
> now. If anyone on your roster is quietly fighting knowledge sprawl, he'd love
> a quick conversation.
>
> **Overview**: https://listhub.globalbr.ai/mockups/ideaflow-memory.html
> **Contact**: jacob@ideaflow.io

# 📋 Targeting notes

**Primary fit:**
- Founder / COO / Chief of Staff / ops leader at **30–100 person companies**
- **Tier 1 signal**: they've tried to maintain a Notion / Confluence / Coda wiki and it's stale
- **Tier 1 signal**: they have a CoS or ops person who is manually summarizing Slack into Google Docs
- **Tier 1 signal**: new hires keep asking the same questions because nothing is written down safely

**Not a fit right now:**
- **< 10 people** — not enough knowledge sprawl yet
- **> 500 people** — enterprise sales motion Jacob is not set up for in v0
- **"We're happy with Notion"** — usually means the knowledge is already dead, soft pass

**Important to mention:**
- **Jacob is not selling anything right now** — these are design partner conversations, free, co-design shape
- **Not asking to ingest everyone's Slack** — design partner scope can be narrow (one workspace, one month, specific channels)
