## Long version (~240 words — blog-post-style forward)

> One of my founders, **Jacob Cole**, is building something I'd love to get in front
> of a few of you. It's called **ListHub Memory** — an automatic company memory layer
> for fast-growing startups.
>
> The premise: in 2026 every company's real source of truth is Slack. That's where
> decisions get made, customer insights land, and processes emerge. But nothing
> reliably captures that memory in a form the team (or an AI agent) can actually
> use. Manual wikis go stale in weeks. Notion is where intentions go to die. AI
> enterprise search gives bad answers because the knowledge was never safely
> written down in the first place.
>
> Jacob's built a novel primitive that unlocks this: **the agent classifies
> visibility per item at write time**, as a first-class API field. Leadership
> decisions stay scoped to leadership. Company-wide SOPs are queryable by everyone.
> Sensitive customer context stays with the people who should see it. No manual
> tagging, no poisoned vault.
>
> The product ingests Slack first (optionally email, Drive, local files), extracts
> structured knowledge continuously, and lets people ask questions from the surfaces
> they already use: a Slack bot, iMessage as a remote control, or their own Claude.
>
> He's running a handful of design partner pilots with 30–100 person companies now.
> If anyone on your roster is quietly fighting knowledge sprawl, he'd love a quick
> conversation.
>
> **Overview**: https://listhub.globalbr.ai/mockups/company-llm-wiki-light.html
> **Contact**: jacob@ideaflow.io

## Notes for the investor

- **Target**: founder / COO / Chief of Staff / ops leader at 30-100 person companies
- **Not a fit**: < 10 people (not enough knowledge sprawl yet), > 500 (enterprise sales motion Jacob is not set up for yet)
- **Tier 1 signal**: they've tried to maintain a Notion / Confluence / Coda wiki and it's stale
- **Tier 1 signal**: they have a CoS or ops person who is manually summarizing Slack into Google Docs
- **Soft pass**: "we're happy with Notion" usually means the knowledge is already dead
- **Jacob is not selling anything right now** — these are design partner conversations, free, co-design shape
